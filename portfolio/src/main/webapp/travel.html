I love to travel, and I really love to travel to desolate places. My dream vacation is to go visit the Tibesti Mountain range in Northern Chad. I've been fascinated by Tibesti ever since I read about it in Dino Buzatti's The Walls of Anagoor. It's a mountain range that almost nobody has visited, even scientists and anthropologists who would love to do research there. That's why most of the photos you'll see of it are from space, and not the ground. How many places the size of Germany can you say that about?

Besides Chad, I've been fortunate enough to visit some other places I've always wanted to visit. On my gap year I lived in Alaska for seven months, working on commercial salmon boats to get me by. I also lived in Colombia for several months, working as an English teacher by day and driving around the city on a cheap motorcycle in my birks by night. I also travelled up the Mekong river system over three months as a student on a cultural immersion trip, which was more exotic but a lot less dangerous.
