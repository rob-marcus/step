I've worked on two major projects. In high school I researched and designed medical exoskeletons (you can read more about it here). My freshman year I studied generating 3D models of art from regular images using computer vision. I called my program Paint By Number. I'm still deeply interested in both, and look forward to working on them more in the future. Painting source: Van Gogh.

The basic idea with Paint By Number was to segment each image by distinct brushstrokes, and then apply a filter to each brushstroke mapping relative pixel energy to grayscale, in turn giving a pseudo-3D representation.
